Escape Area 51: Raid Survival

App Store Link (Image)

A 2-D side-scrolling game featuring randomly generated barriers that players must pass through to earn points. The game is over once players collide with a barrier, and the total score earned is uploaded to a leaderboard. Built with Swift, using the SpriteKit framework.

The Inspiration: During the summer of 2019, users on Facebook created a satirical event titled "Storm Area 51, They Can't Stop All of Us". Because of the absurdity of the event, it went viral and garnered millions of RSVP's [read more]

The Challenge: Releasing a mobile game with the "Raid Area 51" meme being the central theme. Because of the viral nature of the meme and event date of September 2019, I had to develop and publish the app within a self-imposed (less than a month) deadline to remain relevant.

Challenges

  1. Game Assets
    • Truthfully, graphic design is not my strong suit. Because my main focus was to develop and most importantly publish an app on the iOS app store within a month, most, if not all of the assets used in "Escape Area 51" is sourced from royalty free websites
  2. Gameplay Design
    • I decided to use a "endless running game" design, prevalent in mobile games
  3. Score Tracking/Leaderboards
    • To incentivize replay value and competition, I implemented GameKit into the app
      • This also allowed me to avoid paying for alternative servers to host a database with user scores
